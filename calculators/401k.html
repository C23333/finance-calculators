401(k) contribution guide

This calculator estimates how your contributions and employer match could grow over time. Use it to test different savings rates and return assumptions.

Employer match

Matching contributions can be a major boost. Model the match percent and limit described in your plan documents.

Contribution rate

Increasing your contribution rate, even by a small amount, can compound into a large difference over time.

Salary growth

Raises change future contributions. Use a conservative estimate and update as your income changes.

How matching is modeled

  • Your contribution is calculated as a percentage of salary.
  • The employer match applies only up to the match limit you enter.
  • Growth compounds annually based on the expected return.

Adjust inputs for realism

  • Use an expected return that matches your asset mix and fee levels.
  • Check your plan's vesting schedule if employer match is not immediate.
  • Review contribution limits annually since they change over time.

Common planning moves

  • Contribute enough to capture the full employer match.
  • Increase contributions after raises to avoid lifestyle creep.
  • Keep investment costs low and rebalance periodically.
Contribution limits, catch-up rules, and plan fees vary by employer and year. Confirm details with your plan administrator.

401(k) calculator FAQ

How is employer match calculated?

The calculator applies your employer match percentage to your contributions up to the match limit you enter. Contributions above that limit are not matched.

Does this calculator include taxes?

No. It focuses on account growth. Taxes on withdrawals depend on whether your contributions are Roth or traditional.

What if I change jobs?

The projection assumes continuous contributions and the same match policy. Job changes can affect contributions, match, and plan fees.

Should I choose Roth or traditional 401(k)?

It depends on your current tax rate versus your expected retirement tax rate. Many savers use a mix to diversify tax exposure.

About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your current age, retirement age, salary, and contribution details to see how your 401(k) will grow over time. The calculator factors in employer matching and investment returns to project your retirement balance.

The Power of Employer Matching

Employer matching is often one of the highest-return parts of a retirement plan. For example, a match of 50% up to 6% of salary means you should contribute at least 6% to receive the full match.

Contribution Limits

  • Employee limits: Updated annually by the IRS
  • Catch-up rules: Vary by age and plan
  • Total limits: Include employer contributions

Investment Strategy Tips

  • Choose low-cost index funds when available
  • Diversify across stocks, bonds, and international investments
  • Consider target-date funds for automatic rebalancing
  • Review and rebalance your portfolio annually
