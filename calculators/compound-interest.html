About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your initial investment, monthly contribution, interest rate, and investment period to see how your money grows over time. Choose different compound frequencies to see how they affect your returns.

How Compound Interest Works

When your investment earns interest, that interest is added to your principal. In the next period, you earn interest on both your original principal AND the previously earned interest. This creates a snowball effect that accelerates your wealth building.

Key Factors That Affect Your Returns

  • Time: The longer you invest, the more powerful compounding becomes
  • Interest Rate: Higher rates lead to faster growth
  • Compound Frequency: More frequent compounding yields slightly higher returns
  • Regular Contributions: Adding money consistently dramatically increases your final balance

The Rule of 72

A quick way to estimate how long it takes to double your money: divide 72 by your interest rate. At 7% interest, your money doubles in approximately 10.3 years (72 / 7 = 10.3).
