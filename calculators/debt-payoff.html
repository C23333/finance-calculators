Compare the avalanche and snowball methods and see how extra payments change your payoff timeline. The projection assumes steady payments and stable interest rates.

Method choice

Avalanche prioritizes high rates for maximum savings. Snowball prioritizes quick wins to build momentum.

Extra payments

Even small monthly extras can reduce total interest and shorten the payoff period.

Minimum payments

How the calculator works

  • Calculates monthly interest on each balance.
  • Applies minimum payments across all debts.

Choosing a payoff method

  • Avalanche saves the most interest over time.
  • Snowball can improve motivation by clearing small balances sooner.
  • You can switch methods if your needs change.

Before you accelerate payoff

Debt payoff calculator FAQ

Which payoff method saves the most interest?

The avalanche method typically saves the most interest because it prioritizes the highest rates first. The snowball method can help with motivation by paying off smaller balances earlier.

Should I build an emergency fund before paying extra?

Many people set aside a small cash buffer for unexpected expenses before accelerating debt payoff, so they do not rely on new debt when surprises occur.

Do balance transfers or consolidation loans help?

They can help if the effective rate and fees are lower than your current debt, and if the repayment terms fit your budget. Always compare total cost.

Why does my payoff time change?

Payoff time changes when you adjust extra payments, interest rates, or add new debts. The calculator assumes steady payments and rates.

About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Add each of your debts with their name, balance, interest rate, and minimum payment. Then enter any extra amount you can pay monthly and choose your preferred payoff method. The calculator will show you the optimal order to pay off your debts.

Snowball vs Avalanche Method

  • Debt Avalanche: Pay off highest interest rate first. Saves the most money mathematically.
  • Debt Snowball: Pay off smallest balance first. Provides quick wins for motivation.

Both methods work - choose the one that fits your personality. The best method is the one you'll stick with!

Tips for Faster Debt Payoff

  • Always pay at least the minimum on all debts
  • Put all extra money toward one debt at a time
  • Consider balance transfer cards for high-interest debt
  • Avoid taking on new debt while paying off existing debt
  • Celebrate each debt you pay off to stay motivated
  • Use windfalls (tax refunds, bonuses) for extra payments

Frequently Asked Questions

Which method should I choose?

Choose Avalanche if you're motivated by saving money and can stay disciplined. Choose Snowball if you need quick wins to stay motivated or have many small debts.

Should I save or pay off debt first?

Many people build a small cash buffer first, then focus on debt. This helps avoid new debt when unexpected expenses pop up.

What about balance transfer cards?

Balance transfer offers can reduce interest, but watch for fees and the promotional end date. Always compare total cost and your payoff timeline.

How much extra should I pay?

Pay as much extra as you can comfortably afford. Even a modest extra payment can shorten your payoff timeline.
