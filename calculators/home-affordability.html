About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your annual income, monthly debt payments, available down payment, and expected interest rate. The calculator uses the 28/36 rule to determine the maximum home price you can afford while maintaining healthy finances.

The 28/36 Rule

  • 28% Rule: Your housing costs should not exceed 28% of gross monthly income
  • 36% Rule: Total debt payments should not exceed 36% of gross monthly income

Tips for Buying Within Your Means

  • Don't max out your budget - leave room for unexpected expenses
  • Consider all costs: taxes, insurance, maintenance, HOA fees
  • Save for a 20% down payment to avoid PMI
  • Keep an emergency fund separate from your down payment
  • Factor in moving costs and initial home setup expenses

Frequently Asked Questions

How much house can I afford?

A general rule is that your monthly housing costs should not exceed 28% of your gross monthly income. Use this calculator to get a personalized estimate based on your specific financial situation.

What is DTI ratio and why does it matter?

DTI (Debt-to-Income) ratio is the percentage of your gross monthly income that goes toward paying debts. Lenders use this to determine how much you can borrow. Most lenders prefer a DTI of 43% or less.

Should I put 20% down?

A 20% down payment helps you avoid PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) and gives you instant equity. However, many loan programs allow lower down payments if you're willing to pay PMI.
