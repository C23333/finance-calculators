Home affordability guide

This calculator estimates a housing budget based on your income, debts, and down payment. It applies a common affordability guideline to help you set a reasonable target.

Income and debts

Monthly income and existing debts determine how much room you have for housing costs.

Down payment

A larger down payment reduces the loan amount and can improve loan terms.

Non-mortgage costs

Property taxes, insurance, and HOA fees can change affordability significantly.

How the guideline works

  • Uses a front-end and back-end debt ratio as a planning baseline.
  • Compares the housing budget against your current monthly debts.
  • Estimates a max price based on your interest rate and term.

Improve accuracy

  • Use realistic estimates for property taxes, insurance, and HOA fees.
  • Consider your actual monthly budget, not just lender limits.
  • Plan for maintenance, moving costs, and future repairs.

Questions to ask before buying

  • Would the payment feel comfortable if income changes?
  • Do you have a cash buffer after the down payment?
  • Are you prepared for annual tax and insurance increases?
This tool uses a common affordability guideline and provides estimates only. Actual lender requirements and personal comfort levels may differ.

Home affordability calculator FAQ

How much house can I afford?

Affordability depends on your income, debts, down payment, interest rate, and other costs. This calculator uses a common guideline to estimate a reasonable monthly payment.

What guideline does this calculator use?

The calculator uses a common front-end and back-end debt guideline to estimate a housing budget. Actual lender requirements can vary.

What is DTI ratio and why does it matter?

DTI (Debt-to-Income) ratio is the share of your gross monthly income that goes to debt payments. Lenders use it to determine how much you can borrow, but limits vary by program.

Should I put 20% down?

Larger down payments reduce the loan amount and can avoid mortgage insurance, but you should keep enough cash for emergencies and closing costs.

About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your annual income, monthly debt payments, available down payment, and expected interest rate. The calculator uses a common affordability guideline to estimate a reasonable maximum home price.

Affordability Guideline

  • Front-end ratio: Guideline for housing costs vs. gross income
  • Back-end ratio: Guideline for total debt vs. gross income

Tips for Buying Within Your Means

  • Don't max out your budget - leave room for unexpected expenses
  • Consider all costs: taxes, insurance, maintenance, HOA fees
  • Consider a larger down payment to reduce loan costs
  • Keep an emergency fund separate from your down payment
  • Factor in moving costs and initial home setup expenses
