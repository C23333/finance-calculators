Inflation planning guide

Use this calculator to estimate how inflation changes the future cost of goods and services. The results depend on the inflation rate you assume.

Purchasing power

Inflation reduces how much a fixed amount of money can buy over time.

Rate assumptions

Small changes in the inflation rate can meaningfully affect long-term costs.

Category differences

Some expenses rise faster than others, so use realistic assumptions for your situation.

How to choose a rate

  • Use a conservative long-term estimate for planning.
  • Model multiple scenarios to understand a range of outcomes.
  • Update assumptions if the economic environment shifts.

Inflation and savings

  • Savings that do not keep pace with inflation lose real value.
  • Long-term plans should consider after-inflation returns.
  • Use inflation-aware tools for retirement and education planning.

Where inflation matters most

  • Housing, healthcare, and education often drive higher cost growth.
  • Short-term expenses may fluctuate more than long-term averages.
  • Local inflation can differ from national averages.
This tool uses a constant inflation rate. Actual inflation can vary year to year and by category.

Inflation calculator FAQ

What inflation rate should I use?

Use a conservative long-term estimate and test multiple scenarios. Inflation varies by year and by category of spending.

How does inflation affect retirement planning?

Inflation reduces purchasing power over time, so retirement plans should account for higher future costs.

What is the CPI?

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) measures the average change in prices paid by consumers for a basket of goods and services.

Does inflation affect all expenses equally?

No. Different categories like housing, healthcare, and energy can move at different rates over time.

About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Enter the current amount of money, the expected annual inflation rate, and the number of years. The calculator will show you how much that same amount of goods will cost in the future and how much your money's purchasing power will decrease.

Understanding Inflation

Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises, causing purchasing power to fall. Central banks often aim for low and stable inflation, but actual results vary.

Historical US Inflation

  • Long-term trends: Inflation varies by decade
  • Short-term swings: Energy and supply shocks can cause spikes
  • Policy impact: Interest rates and fiscal policy influence inflation

Protecting Against Inflation

  • Invest in stocks: Historically outpace inflation over long periods
  • I Bonds: Government bonds that adjust for inflation
  • TIPS: Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities
  • Real estate: Property values often rise with inflation
