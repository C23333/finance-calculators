Loan payoff strategy guide

Use this calculator to see how extra payments change your payoff date and total interest. The model assumes a fixed rate and consistent payments.

Extra payments

Even modest extra payments can shorten the term because they reduce principal early.

Interest savings

Lower principal reduces the interest charged each month.

Consistency

Regular extra payments often outperform occasional large payments.

How the calculator works

  • Calculates monthly interest based on the remaining balance.
  • Applies your regular payment plus any extra amount.
  • Projects the new payoff timeline and total interest.

Planning tips

  • Confirm that extra payments reduce principal, not future payments.
  • Check for prepayment penalties before accelerating payoff.
  • Balance debt payoff with maintaining an emergency buffer.

Compare options

  • Test different extra payment amounts to see the impact.
  • Compare debt payoff to other financial priorities.
  • Consider refinancing if rates and terms are favorable.

Loan payoff calculator FAQ

How do extra payments help?

Extra payments reduce your principal balance, which lowers interest costs and shortens your loan term.

Should I pay off debt or save?

Many people keep a small emergency buffer first, then prioritize higher-interest debt, while continuing to save steadily.

Are there prepayment penalties?

Some loans have prepayment penalties. Check your loan agreement or lender policy before making extra payments.

How do I make sure extra payments go to principal?

Confirm with your lender that extra payments are applied to principal, not to future payments.

About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your current loan balance, interest rate, monthly payment, and any extra amount you can pay each month. The calculator shows how much time and interest you'll save by making extra payments.

Understanding Your Results

  • Interest Saved: The total amount of interest you'll avoid paying by making extra payments
  • Time Saved: How many months or years earlier you'll pay off the loan
  • New Payoff Time: Your new loan payoff timeline with extra payments
  • Payoff Date: The estimated date when your loan will be fully paid

Strategies for Paying Off Debt Faster

  • Round Up Payments: Round your payment to a higher, manageable amount
  • Bi-Weekly Payments: Pay half your monthly payment every two weeks
  • Lump Sum Payments: Apply tax refunds or bonuses to your principal
  • Refinance: If rates have dropped, refinancing could lower your interest rate
  • Automate Extra Payments: Set up automatic transfers to ensure consistency

Frequently Asked Questions

How do extra payments help?

Extra payments go directly toward your principal balance, reducing the amount of interest you pay over time and shortening your loan term.

Should I pay off debt or save?

Many people keep a small emergency buffer first, then prioritize higher-interest debt while continuing to save steadily.

Are there prepayment penalties?

Some loans have prepayment penalties. Check your loan agreement or contact your lender before making extra payments.

How do I ensure extra payments go to principal?

Contact your lender and specify that extra payments should be applied to principal, not future payments. Some lenders have online options to designate this.
