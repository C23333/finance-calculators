Refinance decision guide

Use this calculator to compare your current loan with a potential refinance. The goal is to see whether the new loan improves your total cost, payment structure, or term.

Rate vs term tradeoff

Lower rates can reduce interest, while shorter terms build equity faster but raise payments.

Closing costs

Refinancing has fees that must be recouped through monthly savings. Compare net savings, not just rates.

Time horizon

The longer you plan to stay in the home, the more time you have to benefit from a refinance.

How break-even works

  • Break-even is the point where cumulative savings exceed closing costs.
  • If you plan to move before break-even, refinancing may not pay off.
  • Use total cost comparisons, not just monthly payment changes.

Reasons to refinance

  • Lower the interest rate or convert to a fixed-rate loan.
  • Shorten the term to reduce total interest paid.
  • Reduce payment volatility or consolidate debt.

Reasons to wait

  • You plan to move soon or sell the home.
  • Closing costs outweigh savings in your time frame.
  • Your credit profile or income is in transition.
This tool provides estimates only. Lender fees, credit factors, and loan programs can change results.

Refinance calculator FAQ

When should I refinance my mortgage?

Consider refinancing when the new loan meaningfully improves your total cost, payment structure, or term. The break-even point is when cumulative savings exceed closing costs.

What is a break-even point in refinancing?

The break-even point is the number of months it takes for your monthly savings to equal the closing costs of refinancing. After this point, you start saving money. Calculate it by dividing closing costs by monthly savings.

What are typical refinance closing costs?

Refinance closing costs vary by lender and location and can include appraisal fees, title insurance, origination fees, and other charges. Some lenders offer no-closing-cost options with different pricing.

Does refinancing always reduce monthly payments?

Not always. Shortening the term or rolling in costs can increase payments. Compare total cost and goals, not just the payment amount.

About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your current loan details (balance, rate, remaining months, and payment) along with the new loan terms you're considering. The calculator will show you potential monthly savings, lifetime savings, and how long it will take to break even on closing costs.

Understanding Your Results

  • Monthly Savings: The difference between your current and new monthly payment
  • Break-Even Point: How many months until your savings exceed closing costs
  • Lifetime Savings: Total amount saved over the life of the new loan minus closing costs

When Should You Refinance?

  • Look for meaningful improvement in total cost or loan terms
  • Plan to stay in the home long enough to reach break-even
  • Consider shortening your term to build equity faster
  • Factor in all closing costs, not just the rate
  • Check if your current loan has prepayment penalties
