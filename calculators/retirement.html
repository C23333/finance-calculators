Retirement planning guide

This calculator projects a savings balance based on your inputs and compares it to a target from the 4% rule. Treat the output as a planning range, not a guarantee.

Time horizon

Years until retirement amplify the impact of contributions and investment returns.

Contribution rate

Small increases can create large differences over long periods due to compounding.

Return assumptions

Use a conservative long-term estimate and remember that yearly returns can vary.

What this calculator includes

  • Growth of your current savings at the expected return you enter.
  • Monthly contributions compounded over time.
  • A retirement savings target based on the 4% rule.

What it does not include

  • Taxes, inflation, investment fees, or sequence-of-returns risk.
  • Social Security, pensions, or other income sources.
  • Changes in spending over time or healthcare costs.

Ways to improve your plan

  • Increase contributions over time, especially after raises.
  • Capture employer matching in workplace retirement plans.
  • Keep investment costs low and rebalance periodically.
  • Update assumptions annually as your goals evolve.
This tool provides estimates for planning. Consider a more detailed plan for major life changes or retirement income strategies.

Retirement calculator FAQ

What does this retirement calculator estimate?

It projects future savings based on your current balance, monthly contributions, and expected return, then compares that projection to a target derived from the 4% rule.

Is the 4% rule guaranteed?

No. It is a planning guideline based on historical studies and does not guarantee future outcomes. Market returns and spending needs can vary.

Does this include Social Security or pensions?

No. This calculator focuses on personal savings. Add Social Security, pensions, or other income sources separately when planning.

How should I choose an expected return?

Use a conservative long-term estimate aligned with your asset mix and fees, and revisit it as your allocation changes.

About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your current age, retirement age, savings, and monthly contribution to see if you're on track for retirement. The calculator uses the 4% rule to determine how much you need to save.

The 4% Rule

The 4% rule is a common planning guideline that estimates sustainable withdrawals over a long retirement. It is not guaranteed. To estimate a target, multiply your desired annual income by 25.

Key Factors in Retirement Planning

  • Time: The earlier you start, the more compound interest works in your favor
  • Contribution Rate: Increase savings over time as your income grows
  • Investment Returns: Long-term results vary by asset mix, fees, and market conditions
  • Social Security: This calculator doesn't include Social Security benefits, which can supplement your savings

Tips for Retirement Success

  • Maximize employer 401(k) matching - it's free money
  • Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth
  • Increase contributions when you get raises
  • Keep investment fees low with index funds
