Key Differences

  • Traditional: Tax deduction now, pay taxes on withdrawals

When to Choose Roth

  • You expect higher taxes in retirement
  • You're early in your career (lower tax bracket)
  • You want tax-free growth
  • You want to avoid RMDs

When to Choose Traditional

  • You expect lower taxes in retirement
  • You're in your peak earning years
  • You need the tax deduction now
  • You're above Roth income limits

Roth vs Traditional Guide

Understanding the Tax Treatment

The fundamental difference between Roth and Traditional accounts is when you pay taxes. Traditional accounts give you a tax break today but you pay taxes on withdrawals. Roth accounts are funded with after-tax dollars but grow and can be withdrawn tax-free.

The Tax Rate Question

The key factor in choosing between Roth and Traditional is comparing your current marginal tax rate to your expected tax rate in retirement. If you expect to be in a higher bracket in retirement, Roth is generally better. If you expect to be in a lower bracket, Traditional is usually better.

2024 Contribution Limits

  • IRA (Roth or Traditional): $7,000 ($8,000 if age 50+)
  • 401(k)/403(b): $23,000 ($30,500 if age 50+)
  • Roth IRA Income Limits: Phase-out begins at $146,000 (single) / $230,000 (married)

Consider Tax Diversification

Many financial advisors recommend having both Roth and Traditional accounts for tax diversification. This gives you flexibility in retirement to manage your tax liability by choosing which accounts to withdraw from.

Other Considerations

  • Required Minimum Distributions: Traditional accounts require RMDs starting at age 73; Roth IRAs do not
  • Early Withdrawal: Roth contributions (not earnings) can be withdrawn penalty-free anytime
  • Estate Planning: Roth accounts can be more beneficial for heirs
  • State Taxes: Consider your state's tax treatment of retirement income
