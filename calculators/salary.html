Salary calculator FAQ

How do I convert hourly to annual salary?

Multiply your hourly rate by hours per week and weeks per year. Adjust hours or weeks for your actual schedule.

What is the difference between bi-weekly and semi-monthly pay?

Bi-weekly pay occurs every two weeks, while semi-monthly pay happens twice per month on set dates.

Does this calculator include taxes?

No. It shows gross pay before taxes and deductions.

Can I adjust hours or weeks worked?

Yes. Update hours per week and weeks per year to reflect your actual schedule.

About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your salary amount and select the pay period (hourly, weekly, bi-weekly, monthly, or annual). Adjust hours per week and weeks per year if needed. The calculator instantly converts your salary to all other time periods.

Common Pay Periods

  • Weekly: 52 paychecks per year
  • Bi-Weekly: 26 paychecks per year (every 2 weeks)
  • Semi-Monthly: 24 paychecks per year (1st and 15th)
  • Monthly: 12 paychecks per year

Salary vs Hourly

Salaried employees typically receive the same pay regardless of hours worked, while hourly employees are paid for actual hours worked and may receive overtime pay depending on local rules.

  • Salaried positions often include benefits like health insurance and retirement plans
  • Overtime rules vary by location and job classification
  • Consider total compensation, not just base salary

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert hourly to annual salary?

Multiply your hourly rate by hours per week, then by weeks per year. Adjust hours or weeks to match your schedule.

What's the difference between bi-weekly and semi-monthly?

Bi-weekly means every two weeks (26 paychecks/year), while semi-monthly means twice a month on set dates like the 1st and 15th (24 paychecks/year).

Does this calculator include taxes?

No, this shows gross pay before taxes and deductions. Use our Take-Home Pay Calculator to see your net pay after taxes.
