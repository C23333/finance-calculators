Savings goal planning guide

Use this calculator to translate a goal and timeframe into a monthly savings target. Adjust the interest rate to reflect where you plan to keep your savings.

Goal amount

Start with a specific target and include any taxes, fees, or buffer you expect to need.

Timeframe

Shorter timelines require higher monthly savings. Extend the timeline if the payment is too high.

Interest assumptions

Use conservative rates for short-term savings and revisit assumptions if rates change.

How to set a realistic plan

  • Base the monthly savings target on your actual budget.
  • Use automatic transfers to stay consistent.
  • Revisit the plan every few months to stay on track.

Common goal types

  • Short-term goals: travel, car repairs, or appliances.
  • Mid-term goals: home down payment or tuition.
  • Long-term goals: major lifestyle changes or large purchases.

Where to keep the money

  • High-yield savings for most short-term goals.
  • CDs for funds you will not need immediately.
  • Investment accounts for long-term horizons with higher risk tolerance.
This tool provides estimates only. Actual results depend on rate changes and your ability to save consistently.

Savings goal calculator FAQ

Should I include interest in my savings goal?

If you plan to keep savings in a yield-bearing account, include a conservative rate. For short timeframes, interest will have a smaller impact.

What if I miss a month of savings?

Missing a month reduces progress. You can extend the timeline or increase future contributions to catch up.

Should I invest for long-term goals?

Long-term goals may benefit from investing, but short-term goals are usually better kept in low-risk savings products.

How do I choose a realistic goal?

Start with a target amount, decide on a timeframe, and test whether the required monthly savings fits your budget.

About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your savings goal amount, current savings, timeframe in months, and expected interest rate. The calculator will show you exactly how much you need to save each month, week, and day to reach your goal.

Automate Your Savings

Set up automatic transfers from your checking to savings account right after payday. This "pay yourself first" approach ensures you save before you spend.

Common Savings Goals

  • Emergency Fund: Several months of essential expenses
  • Down Payment: Varies by loan program and budget
  • Vacation: Varies by destination
  • New Car: Down payment based on affordability
  • Wedding: Set a target based on your priorities

Where to Keep Your Savings

  • High-Yield Savings: Best for emergency funds and short-term goals
  • CDs: Higher rates for money you won't need for a set period
  • Money Market: Combines savings with limited check-writing

Frequently Asked Questions

What interest rate should I use?

Use a conservative rate based on the account you plan to use. Rates change over time, so update your assumptions when needed.

Should I invest my savings instead?

For short-term goals, keep money in low-risk savings. For long-term goals, investing may be appropriate if you can tolerate volatility.

What if I can't save the full amount?

Start with what you can afford and increase over time. Even small amounts add up with compound interest.
