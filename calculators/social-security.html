Understanding Social Security

  • Full Retirement Age: 67 for those born 1960 or later
  • Early Retirement: Benefits reduced ~6.67% per year before FRA
  • Delayed Retirement: Benefits increase 8% per year after FRA until 70
  • AIME: Average Indexed Monthly Earnings from highest 35 years

Social Security Benefits Guide

How Benefits Are Calculated

Social Security benefits are based on your 35 highest-earning years. The Social Security Administration (SSA) calculates your Average Indexed Monthly Earnings (AIME) and applies a formula to determine your Primary Insurance Amount (PIA).

The PIA Formula (2024)

  • 90% of the first $1,174 of AIME
  • 32% of AIME between $1,174 and $7,078
  • 15% of AIME above $7,078

When to Claim Benefits

The decision of when to claim Social Security is personal and depends on factors like health, other income sources, and life expectancy. Claiming early provides income sooner but at a permanently reduced rate. Delaying until 70 maximizes your monthly benefit.

Important Considerations

  • Working while receiving benefits: If you claim before FRA and continue working, benefits may be temporarily reduced
  • Taxation: Up to 85% of benefits may be taxable depending on your total income
  • Spousal benefits: You may be eligible for benefits based on your spouse's record
  • Survivor benefits: Your spouse may receive benefits based on your record after your death
