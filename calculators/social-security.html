About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your current age, birth year, and planned retirement age. Input your average annual earnings over your highest 35 years and current earnings. The calculator estimates your monthly Social Security benefit at different retirement ages.

Understanding Social Security

  • Full Retirement Age (FRA): 67 for those born 1960 or later
  • Early Retirement: Benefits reduced ~6.67% per year before FRA
  • Delayed Retirement: Benefits increase 8% per year after FRA until 70
  • AIME: Average Indexed Monthly Earnings from highest 35 years

2024 Social Security Facts

  • Maximum benefit at FRA: $3,822/month
  • Maximum taxable earnings: $168,600
  • COLA adjustment: 3.2%
  • Social Security tax rate: 6.2%

The PIA Formula (2024)

Your Primary Insurance Amount (PIA) is calculated from your AIME:

  • 90% of the first $1,174 of AIME
  • 32% of AIME between $1,174 and $7,078
  • 15% of AIME above $7,078

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I claim Social Security?

It depends on your health, other income sources, and life expectancy. Claiming early provides income sooner but at a permanently reduced rate. Delaying until 70 maximizes your monthly benefit.

Can I work while receiving benefits?

Yes, but if you claim before FRA and continue working, benefits may be temporarily reduced if you earn above certain limits.

Are Social Security benefits taxed?

Up to 85% of benefits may be taxable depending on your total income. The threshold varies by filing status.
