Student loan repayment guide

Use this calculator to model fixed-payment scenarios and see how extra payments affect payoff time. Results assume a stable interest rate and consistent payments.

Loan type matters

Federal and private loans have different protections, repayment options, and eligibility rules.

Repayment term

Longer terms lower monthly payments but increase total interest paid over time.

Extra payments

Additional principal payments can shorten the schedule and reduce interest costs.

What this calculator assumes

  • A fixed interest rate and consistent monthly payments.
  • No deferment, forbearance, or forgiveness applied.
  • Extra payments go directly toward principal.

Ways to lower total cost

  • Pay more than the minimum when your budget allows.
  • Enroll in autopay if your servicer offers a discount.
  • Consider refinancing only after evaluating federal benefits.
  • Use windfalls to reduce principal faster.

Questions to consider

  • Are you eligible for income-driven repayment or forgiveness programs?
  • Do you expect your income to change materially over the next few years?
  • Is your emergency fund strong enough to handle surprises?
This tool provides estimates only. Confirm repayment options and eligibility with your loan servicer.

Student loan calculator FAQ

Should I refinance my student loans?

Refinancing can lower your interest rate, but it may remove federal protections like income-driven repayment or forgiveness eligibility. Compare total cost and benefits before refinancing.

What are income-driven repayment plans?

Income-driven plans set payments based on income and family size and may offer forgiveness after a qualifying period. Rules and eligibility vary.

How does public service loan forgiveness work?

Public service forgiveness can forgive remaining federal balances after qualifying employment and payments. Eligibility rules are specific, so confirm details with your loan servicer.

Is student loan interest tax-deductible?

In many cases, student loan interest can be deductible subject to income limits and IRS rules. Check current guidance for eligibility.

About This Calculator

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your total student loan balance, interest rate, and select a repayment plan. Add any extra monthly payment you can make to see how much faster you can pay off your loans and how much interest you'll save.

Understanding Your Results

  • Monthly Payment: Your required monthly payment based on the selected plan
  • Total Interest: The total interest you'll pay over the life of the loan
  • Total Cost: Principal plus all interest paid
  • Interest Saved: How much you save with extra payments vs. standard plan

Federal Loan Repayment Options

  • Standard Repayment: Fixed payments with a predictable schedule
  • Extended Repayment: Lower payments with a longer timeline
  • Income-Driven (IDR): Payments based on income and family size
  • Public Service Forgiveness: Potential forgiveness for qualifying employment

Tips to Pay Off Student Loans Faster

  • Make payments during grace period to reduce principal
  • Pay more than the minimum when possible
  • Set up autopay if your servicer offers a discount
  • Apply tax refunds and bonuses to your loans
  • Consider refinancing for lower rates (but lose federal benefits)
  • Explore employer student loan repayment benefits
