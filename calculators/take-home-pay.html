Take-home pay guide

Estimate your net paycheck after taxes and pre-tax deductions. Results are simplified and should be validated against your latest pay stub.

Pre-tax deductions

401(k), HSA, and insurance premiums can lower taxable income and reduce withholding.

Filing status and state

Federal and state withholding depend on your filing status, state rules, and local taxes.

Pay frequency

Weekly, biweekly, and monthly schedules change the per-paycheck amount even if annual pay is the same.

What this calculator includes

  • Federal, state, and payroll tax estimates.
  • Common pre-tax deductions you enter.
  • Breakdowns by pay period and annual totals.

What it does not include

  • Local taxes, bonuses, commissions, or overtime.
  • Special tax credits or itemized deductions.
  • Employer-specific benefits not listed in the form.

Improve accuracy

  • Match inputs to your latest pay stub and benefits elections.
  • Update your filing status and withholdings when life changes.
  • Review the results annually since tax rules change over time.
Take-home pay is an estimate. Tax rules and withholding settings vary by employer and year.

Take-home pay calculator FAQ

Why is my net pay different from the calculator?

Paychecks can differ due to withholding updates, benefit changes, bonuses, or local taxes. Use a recent pay stub to adjust inputs.

Do pre-tax deductions reduce taxes?

Yes. Pre-tax deductions lower your taxable income, which can reduce federal and state taxes depending on the deduction type.

How do bonuses affect take-home pay?

Bonuses are often withheld at different rates than regular pay, so the net amount may be higher or lower than a typical paycheck.

Should I change my W-4?

If you consistently owe taxes or receive large refunds, review your W-4 withholdings. Consider professional guidance for complex situations.

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your gross salary, pay frequency, filing status, and state. Add any pre-tax deductions like 401(k) contributions and health insurance. The calculator will show your net take-home pay after all taxes and deductions.

Tax Withholdings

  • Federal Tax: Based on W-4 settings and taxable income
  • Payroll Taxes: Social Security and Medicare withholding
  • State Tax: Varies by state and local rules

States With No Income Tax

State income tax rules vary widely. Some states do not tax wage income, while others have progressive rates or local taxes. Check current rules for your state.

Tips to Increase Take Home Pay

  • Maximize pre-tax retirement contributions
  • Use HSA/FSA for medical expenses
  • Review your W-4 withholdings annually
  • Consider living in a state with no income tax
  • Take advantage of commuter benefits if available

Frequently Asked Questions

What is FICA tax?

FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act) taxes fund Social Security and Medicare. Rates and wage bases change over time.

How do pre-tax deductions help?

Pre-tax deductions like 401(k) and health insurance reduce your taxable income, lowering your tax burden and increasing your effective take-home pay.

What's the Additional Medicare Tax?

Higher-income earners may owe an additional Medicare tax. Thresholds and rules can change, so verify current guidance.
