Understanding Your Paycheck

Federal Income Tax

Federal income tax is calculated based on your taxable income and filing status. The U.S. uses a progressive tax system with seven tax brackets ranging from 10% to 37%. Your W-4 form determines how much is withheld from each paycheck.

FICA Taxes

FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act) taxes fund Social Security and Medicare:

  • Social Security: 6.2% of wages up to $168,600 (2024)
  • Medicare: 1.45% of all wages, plus an additional 0.9% on wages over $200,000 (single) or $250,000 (married)

Pre-Tax Deductions

Pre-tax deductions reduce your taxable income, lowering your tax burden:

  • 401(k): Up to $23,000 in 2024 ($30,500 if 50+)
  • Health Insurance: Employer-sponsored premiums
  • HSA: Up to $4,150 individual / $8,300 family
  • FSA: Up to $3,200 for healthcare expenses

Tips to Increase Take Home Pay

  • Maximize pre-tax retirement contributions
  • Use HSA/FSA for medical expenses
  • Review your W-4 withholdings annually
  • Consider living in a state with no income tax
  • Take advantage of commuter benefits if available
