## Article Requirements

### Format
- **Word count**: 800-1500 words
- **Tone**: Professional but accessible, avoid jargon or explain it when used
- **Audience**: US-based adults interested in personal finance
- **Style**: Educational, actionable, empowering

### Structure
1. **Headline** (H1)
   - SEO-optimized, includes primary keyword
   - Clear benefit or question format
   - 50-60 characters ideal

2. **Meta Description**
   - 150-160 characters
   - Includes primary keyword
   - Compelling reason to click

3. **Introduction** (1-2 paragraphs)
   - Hook the reader with relevance to their finances
   - State what they'll learn
   - Keep it concise

4. **Main Content** (3-5 sections with H2 headers)
   - Break down the topic with clear subheadings
   - Use bullet points and numbered lists where appropriate
   - Include specific numbers, percentages, or examples
   - Add tables if comparing options or showing data

5. **Key Takeaways** (bullet points)
   - 3-5 actionable points
   - Start each with an action verb

6. **Conclusion** (1 paragraph)
   - Summarize the main message
   - End with an encouraging call to action

### SEO Guidelines
- Primary keyword in: title, first 100 words, at least one H2, conclusion
- Use related keywords naturally throughout
- Internal link opportunities (mark with [CALCULATOR: name] or [ARTICLE: name])
- Write for featured snippets: clear definitions, step-by-step lists, comparison tables

### Content Quality
- Cite specific data points (mark sources with [SOURCE: description])
- Avoid generic advice - be specific and actionable
- Include real-world examples or scenarios
- Address common misconceptions
- Consider different reader situations (income levels, life stages)

### Avoid
- Financial advice that requires professional consultation (add disclaimers)
- Outdated information
- Clickbait or sensationalism
- First-person narrative ("I think...")
- Excessive disclaimers in the body (save for end)
